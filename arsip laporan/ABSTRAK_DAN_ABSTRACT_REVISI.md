# ABSTRAK

Penyebaran hoaks berita berbahasa Indonesia masih menjadi persoalan penting karena informasi keliru dapat muncul dalam bentuk narasi panjang, judul, maupun teks berita yang tampak meyakinkan. Sistem deteksi hoaks yang hanya memberikan label akhir pada tingkat dokumen belum selalu membantu pengguna memahami bagian teks yang perlu dicermati. Penelitian ini bertujuan membangun sistem deteksi hoaks berbasis fine-tuning IndoBERT, menerapkan segmentasi, inferensi, visualisasi, dan agregasi pada runtime tingkat kalimat/paragraf, menerapkan BERTopic untuk pemodelan topik, serta mengintegrasikan model ke dalam sistem web. Dataset aktual berasal dari Kaggle `fjrmhri/dataset-skripsi` yang terdiri atas `CNN.csv`, `Detik.csv`, `Kompas.csv`, dan `TurnBackHoax.csv`. Setelah pembersihan, dataset berjumlah 24.598 data dengan distribusi `not_hoax` 12.645 dan `hoax` 11.953. Metode penelitian meliputi praproses data, stratified random split 70/15/15, oversampling ringan hanya pada training set, fine-tuning `indolem/indobert-base-uncased`, kalibrasi threshold runtime sebesar 0,30 berdasarkan validation set, serta pemodelan topik BERTopic menggunakan sentence embedding, UMAP, HDBSCAN, dan c-TF-IDF. Pada test set dengan threshold 0,30, model memperoleh accuracy 0,996748, precision hoaks 0,998880, recall hoaks 0,994423, F1-score hoaks 0,996646, dan AUC 0,999817. Evaluasi BERTopic menghasilkan 79 topik final, coherence c_v 0,520257, DBCV sampled -0,013278, dan outlier rate 21,90%. Sistem web yang dikembangkan menampilkan verdict dokumen, confidence, highlight kalimat/paragraf, topik global, dan topik per paragraf. Sistem ini diposisikan sebagai alat bantu prediksi berbasis model, bukan sistem verifikasi fakta absolut dan tidak melakukan evidence retrieval terhadap sumber eksternal.

Kata kunci: Deteksi Hoaks, IndoBERT, Fine-Tuning, BERTopic, Pemodelan Topik, Klasifikasi Teks

# ABSTRACT

The spread of Indonesian hoax news remains an important issue because misleading information may appear as long narratives, headlines, or news texts that seem convincing. Hoax detection systems that only provide a document-level label may not sufficiently help users identify which parts of a text require closer attention. This study aims to develop a hoax detection system based on fine-tuned IndoBERT, apply sentence- and paragraph-level segmentation, inference, visualization, and aggregation at runtime, implement BERTopic for topic modeling, and integrate the models into a web-based system. The dataset was obtained from Kaggle `fjrmhri/dataset-skripsi` and consists of `CNN.csv`, `Detik.csv`, `Kompas.csv`, and `TurnBackHoax.csv`. After preprocessing, the dataset contained 24,598 records, with 12,645 `not_hoax` samples and 11,953 `hoax` samples. The method includes data preprocessing, a 70/15/15 stratified random split, light oversampling applied only to the training set, fine-tuning `indolem/indobert-base-uncased`, runtime threshold calibration at 0.30 based on the validation set, and BERTopic modeling using sentence embeddings, UMAP, HDBSCAN, and c-TF-IDF. On the test set using the 0.30 threshold, the model achieved an accuracy of 0.996748, hoax precision of 0.998880, hoax recall of 0.994423, hoax F1-score of 0.996646, and AUC of 0.999817. BERTopic evaluation produced 79 final topics, a c_v coherence score of 0.520257, a sampled DBCV score of -0.013278, and an outlier rate of 21.90%. The developed web system displays document verdicts, confidence scores, sentence/paragraph highlights, global topics, and paragraph-level topics. This system is positioned as a model-based prediction aid, not as an absolute fact-verification system, and it does not perform evidence retrieval against external sources.

Keywords: Hoax Detection, IndoBERT, Fine-Tuning, BERTopic, Topic Modeling, Text Classification
